Starting or running a small business involves many decisions, including what insurance coverage to carry. This educational guide outlines common coverage types to consider.

## General Liability Insurance

Most small businesses benefit from general liability coverage. It may protect against third-party claims for bodily injury, property damage, and advertising injury arising from your operations.

Clients, landlords, and event venues often require proof of general liability coverage before doing business with you.

## Commercial Property Insurance

If you own or lease business property, equipment, or inventory, commercial property insurance may protect against fire, theft, vandalism, and certain weather events.

Home-based businesses should note that homeowners policies often provide limited coverage for business property.

## Business Owners Policy (BOP)

A BOP bundles general liability and commercial property coverage, often at a lower cost than purchasing separately. BOPs are designed for small to mid-sized businesses and may include business interruption coverage.

## Workers' Compensation

If you have employees, your state likely requires workers' compensation insurance. Requirements vary by state regarding minimum employee counts and exemptions.

## Commercial Auto Insurance

If your business owns vehicles or employees drive for work purposes, commercial auto insurance may be necessary. Personal auto policies typically exclude business use.

## Professional Liability (E&O)

Businesses that provide professional advice or services — consultants, accountants, real estate agents, IT professionals — may need professional liability (errors and omissions) coverage.

## Cyber Liability

Businesses that store customer data or rely on digital systems may consider cyber liability insurance to address data breach response costs and related liabilities.

## How to Determine What You Need

Coverage needs depend on:

- Your industry and specific operations
- Number of employees
- Physical locations and property
- Contractual requirements from clients or landlords
- State legal requirements

## Questions to Ask a Licensed Professional

- What coverage is legally required for my business in my state?
- What coverage do my clients or lease agreements require?
- Are there gaps between my current policies and my actual risks?
