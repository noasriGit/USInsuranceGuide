General liability insurance is one of the most common business insurance types. This educational guide explains what it generally covers and who may consider it.

## What General Liability Insurance May Cover

General liability (GL) insurance typically addresses third-party claims related to your business operations. Common coverage areas include:

- **Bodily injury** — if a customer or visitor is injured on your premises or due to your operations
- **Property damage** — if your business activities damage someone else's property
- **Personal and advertising injury** — claims such as libel, slander, or copyright infringement in advertising

Specific coverage, exclusions, and limits depend on your individual policy.

## Who May Consider General Liability Coverage

Many businesses carry general liability insurance, including:

- Retail stores and restaurants
- Contractors and tradespeople
- Professional service providers
- Event organizers and landlords

Even home-based businesses may benefit from GL coverage, especially if clients visit your home office.

## What General Liability Does Not Cover

General liability typically does not cover:

- Employee injuries (workers' compensation may apply)
- Damage to your own business property (commercial property insurance may apply)
- Professional errors (professional liability may apply)
- Auto accidents (commercial auto insurance may apply)

## Questions to Ask a Licensed Professional

- What per-occurrence and aggregate limits are appropriate for my business?
- Are there industry-specific exclusions I should understand?
- Does my GL policy cover my specific operations and locations?
