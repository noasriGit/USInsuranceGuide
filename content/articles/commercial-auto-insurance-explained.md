Commercial auto insurance covers vehicles used for business purposes. This guide explains when businesses may need it and what it generally includes.

## Personal vs. Commercial Auto Insurance

Personal auto policies are designed for individual drivers using vehicles for personal transportation. If you use a vehicle primarily for business — making deliveries, transporting clients, or visiting job sites — a personal policy may not provide adequate coverage.

Commercial auto insurance is designed for business-owned or business-used vehicles.

## Common Coverage Components

Commercial auto policies may include:

- **Liability coverage** — for bodily injury and property damage you cause to others
- **Physical damage coverage** — collision and comprehensive for your vehicles
- **Medical payments** — for injuries to occupants of your vehicles
- **Uninsured/underinsured motorist** — protection against inadequately insured drivers

## Fleet Considerations

Businesses with multiple vehicles may benefit from fleet policies that cover all vehicles under one plan. Fleet policies may offer simplified administration and potential cost efficiencies.
