Many homeowners assume their standard home insurance policy covers all water-related damage. In most cases, that assumption is incorrect. Understanding the difference between home insurance and flood insurance is important for adequate property protection.

## What Homeowners Insurance Typically Covers

Standard homeowners insurance policies generally cover sudden and accidental water damage from sources like:

- Burst pipes
- Appliance overflows
- Roof leaks from storm damage

These are typically covered under dwelling and personal property coverage, subject to policy terms and deductibles.

## What Homeowners Insurance Typically Excludes

Most standard policies explicitly exclude damage caused by:

- **Flooding** — water from rising rivers, storm surge, or surface water accumulation
- **Groundwater seepage** — water entering through foundations
- **Sewer backup** — unless a specific endorsement is added

The distinction between "water damage" and "flooding" is important and often misunderstood.

## What Flood Insurance Covers

Flood insurance is a separate policy, typically available through the National Flood Insurance Program (NFIP) or private insurers. It may cover:

- Structural damage to your home from flooding
- Damage to personal belongings (with separate limits)
- Cleanup and debris removal

Flood insurance has its own waiting periods, limits, and exclusions.

## When to Consider Flood Insurance

Consider flood insurance if:

- Your property is in a FEMA-designated flood zone
- You live near water bodies, even outside mapped flood zones
- Your mortgage lender requires it
- You want protection against low-probability, high-cost flood events

## Questions to Ask a Licensed Professional

- Is my property in a FEMA flood zone?
- Does my homeowners policy have any water-related endorsements?
- What flood coverage limits and deductibles are appropriate for my home?
