Virginia has specific auto insurance requirements that drivers should understand before purchasing or renewing a policy. This educational guide covers general information about coverage mandates and factors to consider.

## Minimum Liability Coverage

Virginia requires drivers to maintain minimum liability insurance or comply with alternative financial responsibility requirements. Minimum limits are set by state law and may change over time.

Always verify current minimum limits with the Virginia Bureau of Insurance or a licensed insurance professional.

## Uninsured and Underinsured Motorist Coverage

Virginia insurers must offer uninsured motorist (UM) and underinsured motorist (UIM) coverage. This coverage may help protect you if you are involved in an accident with a driver who lacks adequate insurance.

## Factors That May Affect Your Premium

Several factors may influence auto insurance premiums in Virginia, including driving record, vehicle type, location, coverage limits, and deductibles selected.

## Questions to Ask a Licensed Professional

Before purchasing auto insurance in Virginia, consider asking about appropriate coverage limits, UM/UIM coverage, deductibles, and available discounts.
