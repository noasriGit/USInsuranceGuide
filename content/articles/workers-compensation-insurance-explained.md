Workers' compensation insurance is a key consideration for employers. This educational guide covers the basics of what it is and why it matters.

## What Workers' Compensation May Cover

Workers' compensation typically provides benefits when employees are injured or become ill due to work-related activities. Benefits may include:

- Medical treatment and rehabilitation expenses
- A portion of lost wages during recovery
- Disability benefits for permanent injuries
- Death benefits for surviving dependents

In exchange, employees generally give up the right to sue their employer for the injury in most circumstances.

## State Requirements Vary

Workers' compensation is regulated at the state level. Requirements differ regarding:

- Minimum number of employees triggering the mandate
- Exemptions for certain industries or business sizes
- Whether coverage must be purchased from a state fund or private insurer

Verify requirements in your state with official resources or a licensed insurance professional.

## Cost Factors

Workers' compensation premiums are typically based on:

- Payroll size and employee classifications
- Industry risk classification codes
- Claims history (experience modification rate)
- State rate filings

## Questions to Ask a Licensed Professional

- Am I required to carry workers' compensation in my state?
- How are my employees classified for rating purposes?
- What steps can I take to improve workplace safety and manage costs?
