Maryland requires drivers to carry auto insurance meeting state minimum standards. This guide provides general educational information about Maryland's auto insurance requirements.

## Minimum Liability Coverage

Maryland mandates minimum liability insurance for all registered vehicles. Standard minimum limits include bodily injury per person, bodily injury per accident, and property damage coverage.

Verify current minimum limits with the [Maryland Insurance Administration](https://insurance.maryland.gov/), as requirements may change.

## Personal Injury Protection (PIP)

Maryland is a PIP state, meaning insurers must offer personal injury protection coverage regardless of fault in an accident. PIP may help cover medical expenses and lost wages up to policy limits.

You may be able to waive PIP coverage in writing, but doing so has implications for how medical expenses are handled after an accident. Discuss PIP options carefully with a licensed insurance professional.

## Uninsured Motorist Coverage

Maryland also requires uninsured motorist coverage as part of standard auto policies. This coverage may protect you if you are hit by a driver without adequate insurance.

## Proof of Insurance

Maryland uses an electronic insurance verification system. Lapses in coverage may result in fines, registration suspension, or other penalties. Maintain continuous coverage and update your insurer promptly when changing vehicles.

## Factors That May Affect Your Premium

Premium factors in Maryland may include:

- Driving history and at-fault accidents
- Vehicle garaging location
- Annual mileage
- Coverage selections and deductibles
- Age and driving experience

## Questions to Ask a Licensed Professional

- What liability limits are appropriate beyond state minimums?
- Should I waive or keep PIP coverage?
- How does uninsured motorist coverage work on my policy?
- What discounts may be available to me?
