Washington, D.C. has its own auto insurance requirements that differ from Virginia and Maryland. This educational guide provides general information for District drivers.

## Minimum Liability Coverage

D.C. requires all registered vehicles to carry minimum liability insurance meeting District standards. Minimum limits cover bodily injury and property damage.

Verify current limits with the DC Department of Insurance, Securities and Banking (DISB).

## No-Fault and Tort Options

D.C. has a unique system regarding personal injury protection and tort options. When you purchase or renew a policy, you may need to make elections that affect how injury claims are handled after an accident.

These elections can have long-term implications. Speak with a licensed insurance professional before making your selection.

## Uninsured Motorist Coverage

Uninsured motorist coverage is an important consideration in the District. Discuss appropriate limits with a licensed professional.

## Factors That May Affect Your Premium

D.C. premiums may reflect urban driving conditions, parking arrangements, vehicle theft rates in your neighborhood, and your coverage elections.

## Questions to Ask a Licensed Professional

- What are the current minimum liability limits in D.C.?
- How do no-fault and tort elections affect my coverage?
- What uninsured motorist limits are appropriate for my situation?
