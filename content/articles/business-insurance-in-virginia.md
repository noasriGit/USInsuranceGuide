Operating a business in Virginia involves understanding the insurance coverage types that may be required or advisable for your operations. This guide provides general educational information.

## Common Business Coverage Types

Virginia businesses may consider several coverage types depending on their operations:

- **General liability insurance** — may cover third-party bodily injury and property damage claims
- **Commercial property insurance** — may protect business property and equipment
- **Workers' compensation** — may be required if you have employees
- **Commercial auto insurance** — for business-owned vehicles
- **Professional liability** — for businesses providing professional services

## Workers' Compensation in Virginia

Virginia generally requires employers with three or more full-time or part-time employees to carry workers' compensation insurance. Certain industries and business structures may have different requirements.

Verify current requirements with the Virginia Workers' Compensation Commission.

## Industry-Specific Considerations

Different industries face different risks. A retail store, a contractor, and a professional services firm may each need different coverage combinations. A licensed insurance professional can help identify coverage gaps.
