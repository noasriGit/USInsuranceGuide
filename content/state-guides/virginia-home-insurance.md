sections:
  Overview: |
    Homeowners insurance is not required by Virginia law, but mortgage lenders typically require it. A standard policy may cover your dwelling, belongings, liability, and additional living expenses after a covered loss.

    See our [Virginia homeowners insurance guide](/blog/homeowners-insurance-in-virginia/) for coverage types, regional risks, and questions to ask an agent.
  Coverage Options to Consider: |
    Typical HO-3 policies include dwelling, other structures, personal property, loss of use, personal liability, and medical payments coverage.

    **Usually excluded:** Flood, earthquake, sewer backup (unless endorsed), and gradual maintenance damage. Flood requires a separate policy — see [home vs flood insurance](/blog/home-insurance-vs-flood-insurance/).
  State Requirements & Regulations: |
    Virginia does not mandate homeowners insurance by statute. Lenders may require coverage for mortgaged properties.

    The [Virginia Bureau of Insurance](https://www.scc.virginia.gov/consumers/insurance/property-casualty-consumer/virginia-homeowners-insurance-guide/) publishes a consumer guide and handles consumer complaints. Insurers and rates are regulated by the SCC.
  Factors That May Affect Your Premium: |
    Location (coastal vs inland), dwelling age and construction, coverage limits, deductibles, claims history, protective devices, and credit-based insurance scores may affect premium.

    Coastal and Tidewater properties may face separate wind or hurricane deductibles.
faq:
  - question: "Is homeowners insurance required in Virginia?"
    answer: "Not by state law, but mortgage lenders typically require it. Without a mortgage, coverage is optional but may protect your home and liability exposure."
  - question: "Does Virginia homeowners insurance cover floods?"
    answer: "Standard policies typically exclude flood damage. Separate flood insurance through the NFIP or a private insurer may be needed."
  - question: "What does a standard Virginia homeowners policy cover?"
    answer: "Most HO-3 policies cover the dwelling and other structures against open perils, personal property against named perils, liability, and loss of use — subject to limits and exclusions."
  - question: "Where can I get help with Virginia homeowners insurance?"
    answer: "The Virginia Bureau of Insurance publishes consumer guides and can assist with complaints. A licensed agent can help with policy-specific questions."
