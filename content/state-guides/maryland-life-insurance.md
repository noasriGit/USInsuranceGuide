  Overview: |
    Life insurance may provide a death benefit to beneficiaries when the insured person dies. Maryland residents can choose from term, whole, universal, and other policy types sold by licensed insurers.

    This page is a general overview — not financial or insurance advice. A licensed professional can help you evaluate options for your situation.
  Coverage Options to Consider: |
    **Term life** — coverage for a set period; typically lower premium than permanent policies.

    **Whole / universal life** — permanent coverage that may include a cash value component; terms vary by policy.

    **Group life** — often offered through employers; may be convertible or portable when employment ends.

    Needs depend on dependents, debts, income replacement goals, and estate planning — not one-size-fits-all.
  State Requirements & Regulations: |
    Maryland does not require individuals to carry life insurance. The [Maryland Insurance Administration](https://insurance.maryland.gov/) regulates insurers, agents, and policy forms sold in the state and handles consumer complaints.

    Verify any company or agent is licensed before purchasing. The MIA publishes a [consumer guide to life insurance](https://insurance.maryland.gov/consumer/documents/publications/lifeinsurance.pdf).
  Factors That May Affect Your Premium: |
    Age, health, tobacco use, coverage amount, policy type, term length, and optional riders may affect cost. Underwriting requirements vary by carrier and product.
