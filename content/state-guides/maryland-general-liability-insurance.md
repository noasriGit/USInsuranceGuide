sections:
  Overview: |
    General liability (GL) insurance addresses third-party claims for bodily injury, property damage, and certain personal/advertising injuries arising from business operations.

    Maryland does not require GL for every business, but landlords, clients, and contracts often do. See our [general liability explainer](/blog/general-liability-insurance-explained/).
  Coverage Options to Consider: |
    **CGL policies** typically cover third-party bodily injury, property damage, and advertising injury claims on business premises or from operations.

    **Not covered:** Your own property, employee injuries (workers' comp), professional errors (E&O), and auto liability (commercial auto).

    Common contractual limits include $1M per occurrence / $2M aggregate — your needs may differ.
  State Requirements & Regulations: |
    No blanket Maryland law mandates GL for all businesses. Requirements often come from:

    - Commercial leases
    - Client and vendor contracts
    - Government procurement
    - Professional licensing boards

    Insurers are regulated by the [Maryland Insurance Administration](https://insurance.maryland.gov/). See also [business insurance in Maryland](/blog/business-insurance-in-maryland/) for state-mandated coverage like workers' comp.
  Factors That May Affect Your Premium: |
    Industry type, revenue, payroll, claims history, coverage limits, location, and operations (subcontractors, public access) may affect GL premium.
