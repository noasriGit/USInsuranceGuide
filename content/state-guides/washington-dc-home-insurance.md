sections:
  Overview: |
    Homeowners insurance is not required by District law, but mortgage lenders typically require it. A standard policy may cover your dwelling, belongings, liability, and additional living expenses after a covered loss.

    The [DC Department of Insurance, Securities and Banking (DISB)](https://disb.dc.gov/page/insurance-tips-homeowners-and-renters) publishes consumer tips for homeowners and renters.
  Coverage Options to Consider: |
    Typical HO-3 policies include dwelling, other structures, personal property, loss of use, personal liability, and medical payments coverage.

    **Usually excluded:** Flood, earthquake, sewer backup (unless endorsed), and gradual maintenance damage. Flood requires a separate policy — see [home vs flood insurance](/blog/home-insurance-vs-flood-insurance/).

    Rowhouses and older structures are common in D.C. — replacement cost vs actual cash value matters when setting dwelling limits.
  State Requirements & Regulations: |
    The District does not mandate homeowners insurance by statute. Lenders may require coverage for mortgaged properties.

    DISB publishes consumer guides, handles complaints, and recommends verifying that insurers are licensed in D.C. before purchasing.

    Properties near the Potomac River and low-lying areas may face elevated flood exposure despite urban location.
  Factors That May Affect Your Premium: |
    Location, dwelling age and construction, coverage limits, deductibles, claims history, protective devices, and credit-based insurance scores may affect premium.

    Bundling auto and home policies or raising deductibles may affect cost — discuss options with a licensed agent.
faq:
  - question: "Is homeowners insurance required in Washington, D.C.?"
    answer: "Not by District law, but mortgage lenders typically require it. Without a mortgage, coverage is optional but may protect your home and liability exposure."
  - question: "Does D.C. homeowners insurance cover floods?"
    answer: "Standard policies typically exclude flood damage. Separate flood insurance through the NFIP or a private insurer may be needed."
  - question: "What does a standard D.C. homeowners policy cover?"
    answer: "Most HO-3 policies cover the dwelling and other structures against open perils, personal property against named perils, liability, and loss of use — subject to limits and exclusions."
  - question: "Where can I get help with D.C. homeowners insurance?"
    answer: "DISB publishes consumer tips and can assist with complaints. A licensed agent can help with policy-specific questions."
