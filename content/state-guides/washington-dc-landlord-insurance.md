  Overview: |
    Landlord insurance (often a dwelling fire or rental property policy) is designed for properties you own but rent to tenants. A standard homeowners policy may not adequately cover a rental property.

    This page is a general overview. A licensed agent can help you match coverage to your property type and lease structure.
  Coverage Options to Consider: |
    **Dwelling coverage** — the rental structure against covered perils.

    **Loss of rent** — income lost when the property is uninhabitable after a covered loss.

    **Landlord liability** — injuries or damage linked to the property you own.

    **Not tenant belongings** — tenants may need their own [renters insurance](/states/washington-dc/renters-insurance/).

    Optional umbrella or flood coverage may be worth discussing separately — especially for properties near the Potomac or in flood-prone zones.
  State Requirements & Regulations: |
    The District does not mandate landlord insurance by statute. Mortgage lenders on rental properties typically require property coverage.

    Landlord-tenant insurance obligations may also appear in lease agreements. Insurers are regulated by [DISB](https://disb.dc.gov/page/insurance).
  Factors That May Affect Your Premium: |
    Property location, age, construction, number of units, occupancy type, claims history, coverage limits, and whether the property is short-term vs long-term rental may affect premium.
faq:
  - question: "Is landlord insurance required in Washington, D.C.?"
    answer: "Not by District law, but lenders on mortgaged rental properties typically require property insurance. Leases may address insurance obligations."
  - question: "How is landlord insurance different from homeowners insurance?"
    answer: "Homeowners policies are for owner-occupied homes. Rental properties often need a landlord or dwelling policy that accounts for tenant occupancy and loss-of-rent coverage."
  - question: "Does landlord insurance cover tenant belongings?"
    answer: "No. Tenants' personal property is generally covered by renters insurance, not the landlord's policy."
  - question: "Does landlord insurance cover flood damage?"
    answer: "Standard property policies typically exclude flood. Separate flood insurance may be needed for flood-prone rental properties."
