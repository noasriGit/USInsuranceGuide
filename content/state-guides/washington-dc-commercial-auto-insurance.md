  Overview: |
    Businesses that own, lease, or regularly use vehicles for work typically need commercial auto insurance rather than personal policies. Personal auto policies often exclude business use.

    See our [commercial auto insurance explainer](/blog/commercial-auto-insurance-explained/) for how commercial policies differ from personal coverage.
  Coverage Options to Consider: |
    **Liability** — bodily injury and property damage you or employees cause while driving for business.

    **Physical damage** — collision and comprehensive on business-owned vehicles.

    **Hired and non-owned auto (HNOA)** — liability when employees drive personal or rented vehicles for work.

    D.C. commercial policies must meet the same minimums as personal auto — **25/50/10** liability plus required uninsured motorist coverage.
  State Requirements & Regulations: |
    Vehicles registered to a business or used primarily for commercial purposes must carry liability insurance meeting [D.C. minimums](/blog/washington-dc-auto-insurance-requirements/).

    UM coverage rules mirror personal auto policies. DC DMV requires proof of insurance for registration.

    For broader business context, see [business insurance in Washington, D.C.](/blog/business-insurance-in-washington-dc/). Compare [Maryland](/blog/maryland-auto-insurance-requirements/) (30/60/15) and [Virginia](/blog/virginia-auto-insurance-requirements/) (50/100/25) if you operate across state lines.
  Factors That May Affect Your Premium: |
    Fleet size, vehicle types, driver records, radius of operation, cargo hauled, coverage limits, and industry classification may affect commercial auto premiums.
faq:
  - question: "When do D.C. businesses need commercial auto insurance?"
    answer: "When vehicles are owned by the business, registered commercially, or used primarily for work. Personal policies commonly exclude business use."
  - question: "What are D.C.'s commercial auto minimum limits?"
    answer: "The same 25/50/10 liability minimums that apply to personal auto policies, plus required uninsured motorist coverage."
  - question: "What is hired and non-owned auto coverage?"
    answer: "HNOA liability covers situations where employees drive personal or rented vehicles for business — it is typically an endorsement, not a separate vehicle policy."
  - question: "Where are D.C. commercial auto rules set?"
    answer: "DISB and DC DMV set insurance requirements; the Compulsory/No-Fault Motor Vehicle Insurance Act defines minimum liability limits."
