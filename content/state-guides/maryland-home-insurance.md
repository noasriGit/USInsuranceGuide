sections:
  Overview: |
    Homeowners insurance is not required by Maryland law, but mortgage lenders typically require it. A standard policy may cover your dwelling, belongings, liability, and additional living expenses after a covered loss.

    See our [Maryland homeowners insurance guide](/blog/homeowners-insurance-in-maryland/) for coverage types, regional risks, and questions to ask an agent.
  Coverage Options to Consider: |
    Typical HO-3 policies include dwelling, other structures, personal property, loss of use, personal liability, and medical payments coverage.

    **Usually excluded:** Flood, earthquake, sewer backup (unless endorsed), and gradual maintenance damage. Flood requires a separate policy — see [home vs flood insurance](/blog/home-insurance-vs-flood-insurance/).

    Coastal and Eastern Shore properties may face separate wind or hurricane deductibles.
  State Requirements & Regulations: |
    Maryland does not mandate homeowners insurance by statute. Lenders may require coverage for mortgaged properties.

    The [Maryland Insurance Administration](https://insurance.maryland.gov/Consumer/Pages/homeownersinsurance.aspx) publishes a consumer guide and handles consumer complaints. Insurers and rates are regulated by the MIA.

    Properties along the Chesapeake Bay and Atlantic coast may face higher wind and flood exposure than inland homes.
  Factors That May Affect Your Premium: |
    Location (coastal vs inland), dwelling age and construction, coverage limits, deductibles, claims history, protective devices, and credit-based insurance scores may affect premium.

    Hurricane, tropical storm, and nor'easter risks vary across Maryland regions.
faq:
  - question: "Is homeowners insurance required in Maryland?"
    answer: "Not by state law, but mortgage lenders typically require it. Without a mortgage, coverage is optional but may protect your home and liability exposure."
  - question: "Does Maryland homeowners insurance cover floods?"
    answer: "Standard policies typically exclude flood damage. Separate flood insurance through the NFIP or a private insurer may be needed."
  - question: "What does a standard Maryland homeowners policy cover?"
    answer: "Most HO-3 policies cover the dwelling and other structures against open perils, personal property against named perils, liability, and loss of use — subject to limits and exclusions."
  - question: "Where can I get help with Maryland homeowners insurance?"
    answer: "The Maryland Insurance Administration publishes consumer guides and can assist with complaints. A licensed agent can help with policy-specific questions."
