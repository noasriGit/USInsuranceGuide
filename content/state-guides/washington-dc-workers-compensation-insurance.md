  Overview: |
    Workers' compensation provides benefits for work-related injuries and illnesses. In Washington, D.C., it is District-mandated for many employers.

    See our [workers' compensation explainer](/blog/workers-compensation-insurance-explained/) and [D.C. business insurance guide](/blog/business-insurance-in-washington-dc/) for details.
  Coverage Options to Consider: |
    Workers' comp is not optional for qualifying employers — coverage is obtained from a licensed carrier, approved self-insurance, or registered PEO.

    It covers employee medical treatment and wage replacement; it does not replace general liability or commercial auto.
  State Requirements & Regulations: |
    **Threshold:** Employers with **one or more employees** must carry coverage. Homeowners employing domestic workers **240+ hours per 13-week quarter** may also need coverage.

    **Exceptions:** Sole proprietors, partners, casual workers, and unpaid volunteers are generally not covered; federal and District government employees have separate programs.

    Administered by the [DC Office of Workers' Compensation](https://does.dc.gov/page/workers-compensation-does).

    Compare: [Virginia](/blog/business-insurance-in-virginia/) requires coverage at **3+ employees** — important if you operate across the DMV.
  Factors That May Affect Your Premium: |
    Payroll, employee job classifications (risk codes), claims history (experience mod), and industry type drive workers' comp premiums. Rates are filed with the state; the Office does not set premiums.

  - question: "Are sole proprietors covered by D.C. workers' comp?"
