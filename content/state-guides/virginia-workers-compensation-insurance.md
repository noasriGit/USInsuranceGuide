  Overview: |
    Workers' compensation provides benefits for work-related injuries and illnesses. In Virginia, it is state-mandated for many employers.

    See our [workers' compensation explainer](/blog/workers-compensation-insurance-explained/) and [Virginia business insurance guide](/blog/business-insurance-in-virginia/) for details.
  Coverage Options to Consider: |
    Workers' comp is not optional for qualifying employers — coverage is obtained from a Virginia-licensed carrier, approved self-insurance, or registered PEO.

    It covers employee medical treatment and wage replacement; it does not replace general liability or commercial auto.
  State Requirements & Regulations: |
    **Threshold:** Employers who **regularly employ more than two** employees (3+) must carry coverage. Part-time, seasonal, and subcontractor employees performing the same trade may count.

    **No waivers** for qualifying employers. Virginia-specific coverage is required for work performed in Virginia.

    Administered by the [Virginia Workers' Compensation Commission](https://www.workcomp.virginia.gov/content/employers).

    Compare: Maryland and D.C. require coverage at **1+ employee** — important if you operate across the DMV.
  Factors That May Affect Your Premium: |
    Payroll, employee job classifications (risk codes), claims history (experience mod), and industry type drive workers' comp premiums. Rates are filed with the state; the Commission does not set premiums.
faq:
  - question: "When is workers' compensation required in Virginia?"
    answer: "When an employer regularly employs more than two part-time or full-time employees — meaning three or more."
  - question: "Can Virginia employees waive workers' comp?"
    answer: "No. For employers who meet the threshold, coverage is mandatory and employees cannot be excluded."
  - question: "Do subcontractors count toward the employee threshold?"
    answer: "Yes — subcontractor employees performing the same trade or fulfilling a contract may count when determining whether you meet the 3+ employee threshold."
  - question: "Who administers Virginia workers' compensation?"
    answer: "The Virginia Workers' Compensation Commission oversees compliance, claims, and employer requirements."
