  State Requirements & Regulations: |
    **Workers' compensation:** Employers with more than two employees (3+) must carry coverage. Subcontractor employees performing the same trade may count. No waivers for qualifying employers. Administered by the [Virginia Workers' Compensation Commission](https://www.workcomp.virginia.gov/content/employers).

    **Commercial auto:** Business vehicles must meet Virginia [auto minimums](/blog/virginia-auto-insurance-requirements/) (50/100/25).

    **General liability:** Not mandated for all businesses, but common in leases and contracts.
  Factors That May Affect Your Premium: |
    Industry classification, payroll, claims history, number of employees, vehicles, location, and coverage limits selected. Workers' comp rates vary significantly by trade.
faq:
  - question: "Is workers' compensation required in Virginia?"
    answer: "Yes, for employers who regularly employ more than two part-time or full-time employees — meaning three or more. Coverage is mandatory with no waivers for qualifying employers."
  - question: "Is general liability required for Virginia businesses?"
    answer: "Virginia does not require GL for all businesses, but landlords, clients, and licensing boards often require proof of coverage."
  - question: "Do I need commercial auto insurance in Virginia?"
    answer: "If your business owns or primarily uses vehicles for work, you likely need commercial auto rather than personal coverage."
  - question: "Where do I verify Virginia business insurance rules?"
    answer: "Workers' comp: Virginia Workers' Compensation Commission. Auto and general insurance: Virginia Bureau of Insurance."
