  Overview: |
    Maryland requires liability insurance on every registered vehicle. The Motor Vehicle Administration (MVA) verifies coverage electronically.

    This page is a brief state overview — not a full requirements guide. See our [Maryland auto insurance requirements article](/blog/maryland-auto-insurance-requirements/) for limits, PIP waivers, penalties, and proof-of-insurance details.
  Coverage Options to Consider: |
    **Required:** Bodily injury and property damage liability (30/60/15), plus uninsured/underinsured motorist coverage at matching minimums.

    **PIP:** Insurers must offer at least $2,500 in personal injury protection unless you sign an affirmative written waiver. Limited PIP options may also be available.

    **Common optional coverages:** Collision, comprehensive, and higher liability limits. Maryland is generally an at-fault state for bodily injury claims.
  State Requirements & Regulations: |
    Minimum liability is **30/60/15**. UM/UIM bodily injury and property damage minimums mirror liability limits.

    MVA may impose penalty fees for lapses — $150 for the first 30 days without verified coverage and $7 for each additional day. Registration suspension and an FR-19 certificate may be required for reinstatement.

    Neighboring [Virginia](/blog/virginia-auto-insurance-requirements/) (50/100/25) and [D.C.](/blog/washington-dc-auto-insurance-requirements/) set different minimums — your policy must meet Maryland rules where the vehicle is registered.
  Factors That May Affect Your Premium: |
    Insurers may consider driving record, age, vehicle type, garaging location, mileage, coverage limits, deductibles, PIP election, and credit-based insurance scores where permitted.

    See [why car insurance rates may increase](/blog/why-did-my-car-insurance-go-up/) for common premium trends.
faq:
  - question: "What are Maryland's minimum auto insurance limits?"
    answer: "Minimum liability is $30,000 per person, $60,000 per accident for bodily injury, and $15,000 per accident for property damage (30/60/15). UM/UIM minimums mirror these limits."
  - question: "Is personal injury protection (PIP) required in Maryland?"
    answer: "Insurers must offer at least $2,500 in PIP, but you may waive it through an affirmative written waiver on the form required by the Maryland Insurance Administration."
  - question: "Does Maryland require uninsured motorist coverage?"
    answer: "Yes. UM/UIM bodily injury and property damage minimums must match your liability limits."
  - question: "What happens if my Maryland auto insurance lapses?"
    answer: "MVA may charge penalty fees, suspend registration, and require an FR-19 certificate showing continuous coverage before reinstatement."
